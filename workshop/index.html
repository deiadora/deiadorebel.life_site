Quantum Currency Workshop · Step 1 · Coherence · Torus

The torus doesn't distinguish between work money and life money.
It just asks: is the loop closed?

In quantum mechanics, coherence describes the condition of a system whose components are operating in phase — generating its own sustained energy rather than drawing it from an external source. The torus is the geometry of that condition: a self-sustaining loop where energy flows out, curves back, and returns to origin without loss.

"A CEO who is profitable on paper but personally depleted is running a broken torus. The audit reveals which one."

Before we build any model or governance structure, we map what's actually flowing. Personal and business finances together — because the system is one system, even when we account for them separately.

Four torus states — which one is closest to now?

Coherent

The loop is closed

Revenue funds operations funds growth. Personal income covers life without depleting the business. The torus is spinning.

Bound state

Dependent on input

Survival requires external fuel — investment, a partner's income, debt, the next contract. The loop can't close on its own yet.

Leaking

Energy exits the system

Revenue arrives but doesn't return to origin. Unclear costs, underpricing, scope creep, unpaid labour — the torus has holes.

Frozen

Motion has stopped

The system isn't moving. Paralysis, avoidance, overwhelm — the financial picture exists somewhere but no one is looking at it.

Which of these is closest to where you are right now — in the business, and in your life?

Both answers matter. Neither is wrong. Honest is the only requirement.

Why personal and business together

Most financial frameworks treat personal and business as separate systems. The torus doesn't — because they aren't. A founder who is underpaying themselves to keep the business afloat is not running a coherent business. They are subsidising it. A team where everyone is personally stretched has a collective ground state of scarcity, regardless of what the P&L shows. The audit maps both because coherence is a property of the whole system, not one ledger.

What bound state actually looks like

In physics, a bound state is when a system cannot reach its ground state independently — it requires energy from outside the bond to ionise, to become free. In a team, bound state shows up as: the runway conversation that happens every quarter, the personal savings that quietly fund the business, the contract that has to close or the whole thing stalls. Bound state is not failure. It is a phase — one that has a physics-described exit condition: enough energy from outside the bond to exceed the binding energy. The audit names where that energy needs to come from and what it would take to close the loop internally.

Torus state signals — what to listen for

Coherent signals

The team can describe the financial picture without a spreadsheet in front of them. Personal and business income feel proportionate. There's a cushion — not necessarily large, but present. Decisions get made without financial paralysis.

Bound state signals

"We just need to land this one client." Personal savings quietly entering the business. The founder's salary is the first thing cut. Survival language: runway, burn rate, bridge.

Leaking signals

Revenue feels inconsistent despite consistent work. Scope creep accepted without repricing. Unpaid labour normalised — "I'll sort the invoices later." No one quite knows where the money goes.

Frozen signals

The accounts haven't been opened in weeks. The question "how are we actually doing?" produces discomfort rather than a number. Financial decisions get deferred indefinitely.

What the Sower lens adds here

The torus audit is Step 1 — it runs on the ground prepared in Step 0. A room that is still on the path (information doesn't land) will produce a surface-level audit: numbers without honesty. A room in good soil will name the bound state or the leak directly, without defensiveness. The quality of the audit is a direct readout of the soil quality from Step 0. If the audit feels thin, return to the field before proceeding.

The insight this step tends to produce

The most common revelation in the Step 1 audit is not a number. It is the recognition that the business and the person running it have been treated as separate systems when they are not. The founder who has been "protecting the business" from their personal financial reality — or protecting their personal life from the business's — has been managing two broken half-toruses instead of one coherent whole. Naming this is the beginning of coherence, not the end of it.

This is the current state audit. It is not a judgement of where you are — it is a map. A map is only useful if it's honest. Approximate numbers are fine. Directional honesty is what matters. Both your personal and business finances belong here, together, because the system is one system.
1

Money in — the full picture

List every source of income — business revenue, salary, investments, support from others, savings drawdown, anything. Approximate monthly figures. Include sources you'd rather not name — those are often the most important ones.

Business income

Revenue, retainers, contracts, recurring income. What actually arrives each month?

Personal income

Salary, freelance, investments, partner income, savings being drawn, any other source.

2

Money out — where does the energy go?

Business costs, personal living expenses, debt servicing, anything leaving the system monthly. Again — approximate is fine. Include things you pay for the business personally that don't appear on the books.

Business costs

Overheads, tools, people, services. What does it cost to operate each month?

Personal costs

Rent, food, debt, life. What does it cost to be you each month?

3

Where does the energy leak?

What are you doing that doesn't return energy to the system — work that's underpriced, scope that keeps expanding, time that goes unpaid, decisions deferred because the financial picture is uncomfortable to look at?

4

The torus verdict

Based on what you've just mapped — which state is closest to where this system is right now? Select all that apply. More than one can be true simultaneously.

5

What would closing the loop require?

If the torus is not yet self-sustaining — what is the binding energy? What would it take for this system to generate its own coherence? Name one concrete thing, however small.

The Sower question at Step 1

Return to the lens from Step 0: what kind of ground is this system growing in right now?

The torus audit doesn't change the soil. It makes the soil visible. A bound state in good soil is already becoming something else — the energy to exceed the binding energy is accumulating. A leaking system in thorny ground keeps leaking because there's no space to find the holes.

The map you've just made is the beginning of coherence. The system sees itself. That is the first condition for change.
